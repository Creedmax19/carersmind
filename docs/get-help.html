Quick Tips to Cope:

  • Take micro-breaks: Even 5 minutes of deep breathing or stretching can reset your nervous system.
  • Set boundaries: It's okay to say no when you feel overwhelmed.
  • Talk it out: Sharing your feelings with someone you trust often brings relief.
  • Write things down: Keeping a simple journal can help you process difficult days.

üü† 2. Emergency Help and Crisis Lines

If you ever feel you can't keep yourself or someone else safe, please call 999 or go to your nearest A&E.

24/7 Mental Health Helplines:

  • Samaritans: 116 123 (free, confidential)
  • Shout Text Line: Text SHOUT to 85258
  • Mind Infoline: 0300 123 3393

If you are caring for someone with a mental health condition and you're worried, you can also contact your local NHS crisis team (find details at NHS urgent mental health helpline).

üü£ 3. Self-Care for Carers

Remember: Self-care is not selfish.

  • Stay connected: Loneliness increases stress. Make time for a friend or community group.
  • Eat well: Nourishing your body helps you keep going.
  • Move your body: Even gentle exercise helps clear your mind.
  • Get support: Asking for help is a sign of strength, not weakness.
